\section{Content Heading}
\vspace{-3mm}
To regulate the DC bus voltage at the terminals of DC-DERs, an appropriate converter model is required for controller design. In this thesis, a boost converter is employed to interface the DC DERs with the DC bus. The basic block diagram of the DC DER integrated through a boost converter is shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:dcdc_converter_block}.
\begin{figure}[b!]
     \centering
     \includegraphics[width=0.55\linewidth]{imgs/chapter_2/content_1/fig_1.png}
     \caption{Basic block diagram for boost converter based DC-DERs}\label{fig:dcdc_converter_block}
\end{figure}

\vspace{-3mm}
The modeling of the converter is performed by taking the average of the system during turn-on and turn-off cases of the SW as follows
\begin{figure*}[h]
\begin{minipage}{0.48\textwidth}
    \centering\textbf{During SW is turn-on}
    \begin{align}
         V_{in} &= L^{con} \cdot \frac{dI_{L}^{con}}{dt} \label{dcdc_converter_eq2}\\
        C_{o} \cdot& \frac{dV^{dc}}{dt} = - \frac{V^{dc}}{R_{o}} \label{dcdc_converter_eq3}
    \end{align}
    \end{minipage}%
    \hfill%
    \begin{minipage}{0.48\textwidth}
    \centering\textbf{During SW is turn-off}
    \begin{align}
        V_{in} - V^{dc} &= L^{con} \cdot \frac{dI_{L}^{con}}{dt} \label{dcdc_converter_eq4} \\
        C_{o} \cdot \frac{dV^{dc}}{dt} &= I_{L}^{con} - \frac{V^{dc}}{R_{o}} \label{dcdc_converter_eq5}
    \end{align}
\end{minipage}
\end{figure*}

\vspace{-6mm}
Now using ~\eqref{dcdc_converter_eq2}, ~\eqref{dcdc_converter_eq3}, ~\eqref{dcdc_converter_eq4}, and ~\eqref{dcdc_converter_eq5}, the average model is developed as follows:
\begin{align}
    V_{in} - V^{dc} &\left( 1 - D^{con}\right) = L^{con} \frac{dI_{L}^{con}}{dt} \label{dcdc_converter_eq6} \\
    C_{o} \cdot \frac{dV^{dc}}{dt} &= I_{L}^{con}\cdot \left( 1 - D^{con}\right)  - \frac{V^{dc}}{R_{o}} \label{dcdc_converter_eq7}
\end{align}
Taking the small perturbation, the state space model is developed as follows:
\vspace{-3mm}
\begin{equation}
    \label{dcdc_converter_eq8}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    \dot{\Delta I_{L}^{con}} \\ 
    \dot{\Delta V^{dc}}
    \end{bmatrix}
    =
    \begin{bmatrix}
    0 & \frac{-(1-D^{con})}{L^{con}} \\
    \frac{1-D^{con}}{C_{o}} & \frac{-1}{C_{o}R_{o}}
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    \Delta I_{L}^{con} \\ 
    \Delta V^{dc}
    \end{bmatrix}
    +
    \begin{bmatrix}
        \frac{1}{L^{con}} & \frac{V^{dc}}{L^{con}} \\
        0 & \frac{-I_{L}^{con}}{C_{o}}
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
        \Delta V_{in} \\
        \Delta D^{con}
    \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    \label{dcdc_converter_eq9}
    \Delta V^{dc} = 
    \begin{bmatrix}
        0 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}
    \begin{bmatrix}
        \Delta I_{L}^{con}\\
        \Delta V^{dc} 
    \end{bmatrix}
\end{equation}
